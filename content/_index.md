---
title: "The Woman's Journal Digital Archive"
description: "A digital archive of The Woman's Journal, the leading newspaper of the women's suffrage movement."
---

*The Woman's Journal* was founded in Boston, Massachusetts in January 1870 by Lucy Stone and Henry B. Blackwell. Published weekly for nearly fifty years, it became the most influential newspaper of the American women's suffrage movement, advocating for voting rights, educational access, labor reform, and the full equality of women in public life.

This digital archive presents selected issues from the journal's first year of publication (1870), demonstrating how structured metadata and digitized primary sources can be combined to support historical research. Each issue record links directly to the full digitized text at the Internet Archive.
