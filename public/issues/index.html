Jan 8
Vol. 1, No. 1 January 8, 1870 Boston, MA

The Woman's Journal - January 8, 1870

suffrage inaugural women's rights

This is the inaugural issue of The Woman’s Journal, launched on January 8, 1870 from offices in Boston, Massachusetts. The first issue set out the paper’s founding mission: to advocate for women’s suffrage and the full equality of women in American civic life. The Woman's Journal - March 26, 1870

suffrage education reform

Volume 1, Issue 12 of The Woman’s Journal covers ongoing suffrage campaigns and features reporting on the state of women’s education and access to higher learning. Articles in this issue address legislative efforts in several states and the connection between educational reform and women’s full citizenship. Vol. 1, No. 36 September 10, 9870 Boston, MA

The Woman's Journal - September 10, 1870

suffrage politics 15th Amendment

Volume 1, Issue 36 of The Woman’s Journal grapples with the aftermath of the Fifteenth Amendment’s ratification, which granted voting rights to Black men but explicitly excluded women. The issue reflects on the fractured relationships within the broader reform movement and reaffirms the paper’s commitment to universal suffrage. The Woman's Journal - December 3, 1870

suffrage year in review progress

Volume 1, Issue 48 of The Woman’s Journal offers a retrospective on the paper’s first year of publication, surveying the state of the suffrage movement at the close of 1870. The editors reflect on legislative gains and setbacks, celebrate the paper’s growing readership, and look ahead to the campaigns of 1871.
